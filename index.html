Co-Designing a Smartwear Revolution is an international research project led by the University of Alberta and funded by Canada’s New Frontiers in Research Fund. Through collaborations between researchers, artists, designers, technologists, and People with Lived Experience (PLEX), the project explores the creative and social possibilities of smartwear technologies and intelligent fibres designed to support movement, care, and everyday life. With sensing, actuating, and battery-power capabilities built directly into the fibres, the Smartwear Revolution project aims to adapt clothing stiffness and shape to provide physical support and movement augmentation as needed. These smartwear fibres incorporate intelligent technologies that identify user intent and dynamically adjust the fabric’s properties to support trunk, arm, and leg movements during daily activities such as self-care, reaching, and carrying loads. The theme of SUPPORT thus mirrors the technical goals of the Smartwear Revolution project which include providing trunk support by 2028 and augmentation of arm and leg movement by 2031.

Support shapes everyday life in many different ways: through people, environments, objects, technologies, routines, and forms of care. It can be physical, emotional, social, cultural, or technological, intentionally designed or developed through lived experience and adaptation.

Within the context of smartwear and assistive technologies, support is often understood as something that helps movement, access, safety, communication, or participation. At the same time, support is not a fixed or universal idea; it is shaped by relationships, systems, values, and the diverse experiences of those who use and create it. Support comes in different textures and temporalities.

We invite artists, performers and designers to engage with the concept of support in its many forms and meanings. How is support experienced, negotiated, designed, shared, or resisted? What kinds of support are visible or invisible? How does it feel, taste, smell, move, stretch, confine, liberate? How do communities create systems of care and access for one another? And how might art, design, and technology imagine new possibilities for support shaped by lived experience?

We also invite artists to creatively engage with some of the specific technical questions the research teams are currently encountering, like:

  • How is trunk support defined?
  • How and where should smartwear be anchored on the body?
  • How should recharging smart garments work?
  • How should intention be detected?
  • How to prevent the loss of agency and AI rage?

Below, we explain the context for this festival, who is eligible to apply, possible formats for making work, what to expect in the application process, and ways to ask for assistance should you need it. We look forward to reading your proposal!

The context

In order to spark conversation, critique and co-design with the communities that our smart garments aim to serve, the Smartwear Revolution’s Art & Design Working Group is organizing two international art and technology festivals. The first of these, around the overarching theme of SUPPORT, is slated for 2028, and will likely feature exhibitions, dance or theatre performances, live dress or fashion events, creative research presentations, open lab activities, and public discussions in both in-person and online formats. A second festival, with a theme to be decided, is planned for 2031.

Festival activities will be free of charge, and featured artists will receive fees for their work. In some cases, like commissions and artist residencies, additional production and material fees may be offered. Other resources, like rehearsal space, studio space, or facilities may be available.

Who is eligible to apply?

We are particularly interested in applicants with lived or professional experience related to muscular and skeletal weakness, aging, caregiving, rehabilitation, mobility, chronic illness, and healthcare. This includes, but is not limited to, older adults, healthcare practitioners, caregivers, and people with lived experience of disability or chronic illness. We welcome applications from artists and artist-researchers of all abilities whose work engages with the themes of this project.

Applicants should be able to demonstrate past experience in artmaking, design or performance and will be asked to share documentation of previous work.

Although we look forward to working with some local artists, applicants need not be physically present. There are no restrictions in terms of nationality, geographical location, age, gender, sexuality, religion, or physical and intellectual disability. Student artists, artist collaborators and collectives are welcome to apply, though honoraria may differ in some situations.

Possible formats for your proposals

We are interested in proposals to include works that have already been made, works-in-progress, and new work. We strongly encourage artists to engage with the research team and can facilitate meetings and lab tours (either virtual or in person). For more information on the labs and technologies that you could work with, please feel free to contact us.

We are also facilitating two artistic residencies in Spring 2027, where artists will engage with one or more of the laboratories, technologies and research methods in our Smartwear Revolution project, and make work in response to these engagements.

We are committed to hybrid access to all art works, performance and fashion events, and look forward to collaborating with makers to devise exciting ways for artworks to exist online.

What to expect if you apply

The application process is in two stages. The first stage is this Expression of Interest (EoI), where you tell us about yourself and your practice, and pitch ideas about a possible project you’d like to propose. This is meant to be fairly simple and not overly detailed or labor-intensive.

If your EoI is selected to move to stage two, we will meet with you to discuss your ideas, and ask you to complete a longer and more detailed application, where you will specify what you intend to make or contribute. We will provide a small honorarium or gift card if preferable, to those who complete the full application.

EoIs are due on 15th December 2026 and will be reviewed by an interdisciplinary committee from the Smartwear Revolution team. Successful EoIs will be announced on 15th January with full applications due 15th February.

We will accept EoIs after these dates but due to the complexity of contracts we cannot guarantee inclusion in the 2028 festival.
